# Openness (O)

## Technical Definition
Openness to Experience (part of the Big Five OCEAN model) reflects the degree of intellectual curiosity, creativity, and preference for novelty and variety. In language, it manifests through abstract thinking, complex vocabulary, and willingness to explore unconventional ideas.

## Prompt Engineering Guidelines
When evaluating for Openness, look for:
- **High O:** Use of metaphors, abstract concepts, and sophisticated vocabulary. Willingness to consider multiple perspectives. Expression of curiosity and imagination.
- **Low O:** Focus on concrete, practical, and traditional ideas. Use of simple, direct language. Avoidance of ambiguity or speculative thought.

**Evaluation Prompt snippet:**
"Rate the text from 0.0 to 1.0 on Openness (O). 1.0 is highly curious, abstract, and intellectually adventurous. 0.0 is practical, concrete, and traditional."

## Scoring Examples
- **1.0 (High):** "We should explore the philosophical implications of this algorithm, treating it not just as code, but as a digital mirror to human cognition."
- **0.5 (Neutral):** "I'm interested in how this program works and I think we could try some new features to make it better."
- **0.0 (Low):** "The program performs the required calculations as specified in the manual. We should stick to the established procedure."
